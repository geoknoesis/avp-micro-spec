AVP-Micro Disputes defines the reverse value-flow for [[AVP-MICRO]]: returning value to a payer, whether voluntarily (a Refund) or adversarially (a Dispute that is upheld — a chargeback), and the dispute lifecycle that governs the adversarial case. It reuses the [[AVP-MICRO]] economic terms and the [[DSA]] identity and securing layers; every object is secured with the mandatory ecdsa-jcs-2022 cryptosuite.

Introduction

"Refund", "reversal", and "chargeback" are not three parallel objects: they are the same reverse value-movement reached by different triggers. A Refund is decided voluntarily by the payee; a chargeback is a Dispute that is upheld; a Reversal is the wallet-signed settlement fact that actually moves value back, which both paths produce. This specification therefore models two trigger paths converging on one settlement record.

State is derived from the set of signed records, not stored in a mutable field. Each lifecycle transition is the creation of a new signed, append-only object. This lets the payer, payee, and an optional arbiter each sign their own records independently.

Conformance

As well as sections marked as non-normative, all authoring guidelines, diagrams, examples, and notes in this specification are non-normative. Everything else is normative.

The key words MUST, MUST NOT, SHOULD, MAY, and OPTIONAL in this document are to be interpreted as described in [[RFC2119]] [[RFC8174]].

Disputes objects MUST reuse the [[AVP-MICRO]] terms amount, currency, payer, payee, status, settlementRef, execution, and timestamp rather than redefining them.

Data model

Every object is secured with a Data Integrity proof using the ecdsa-jcs-2022 cryptosuite (see [[DSA]]). Every reference to a payment or dispute object MUST include both the object's IRI and a *Digest equal to the JCS content digest of the referenced (signed) object, pinning it to that exact object.

Refund

A payee-signed voluntary return-of-value intent.

A Refund MUST reference the PaymentReceipt being refunded (receipt + receiptDigest) and MUST be signed by the payee. Its amount MUST be greater than zero and MUST NOT exceed the referenced charge. Multiple partial refunds MAY reference the same original, provided the cumulative settled return does not exceed the original amount. The reason MUST be a concept IRI (see Reason codes).

Dispute

A payer-signed case contesting a prior charge.

A Dispute MUST be signed by the payer and MUST reference at least one of the contested objects (receipt, execution, or authorization), each with its *Digest. Its disputedAmount MUST NOT exceed the original charge. It MAY propose an arbiter DID for escalation and a respondBy deadline.

DisputeEvidence

An append-only, party-signed evidence or representment record.

A DisputeEvidence record MUST be signed by the party named in submittedBy, whose role (payer or payee) MUST match. Each record carries a per-dispute sequence that MUST be unique. The evidence artifact itself is out of scope; the record carries only its contentDigest and an optional uri.

DisputeResolution

A signed decision: upheld, rejected, partial, or withdrawn.

A DisputeResolution carries an outcome and a resolvedAmount (which MUST NOT exceed the disputedAmount, and MUST be zero for rejected and withdrawn). A withdrawn resolution MUST have resolverRole payer. An arbiter resolution MUST carry supersedes + supersedesDigest referencing the payee resolution it overrides, and its signer MUST equal the dispute's arbiter.

Reversal

A wallet-signed settlement fact recording value returned to the payer.

A Reversal MUST be signed by the wallet and MUST reference exactly one trigger: a refund (when cause is refund) or a resolution (when cause is dispute), each with its digest. A dispute-caused Reversal's referenced resolution MUST have outcome upheld or partial. The amount MUST equal the trigger's amount (or be less when status is partial). When present, the original forward execution SHOULD be referenced.

ReversalAcknowledgement

An OPTIONAL payer-signed confirmation of receipt.

A ReversalAcknowledgement MUST be signed by the payer and reference the reversal (+ reversalDigest) whose funds were received.

Dispute lifecycle

Voluntary:   Refund (payee) -> Reversal cause=refund (wallet) -> [Ack (payer)]

Adversarial: Dispute (payer)
               -> OPENED -> [DisputeEvidence* (payer/payee)]
               -> UNDER-REVIEW -> DisputeResolution (payee)
                    rejected  -> CLOSED
                    upheld/partial -> Reversal cause=dispute (wallet) -> [Ack] -> CLOSED
                    payer escalates -> DisputeResolution (arbiter, supersedes payee) [BINDING]
                                         -> rejected -> CLOSED
                                         -> upheld/partial -> Reversal -> CLOSED
               -> WITHDRAWN (DisputeResolution role=payer, outcome=withdrawn) -> CLOSED

Reason codes

Reason values are concept IRIs in the extensible SKOS scheme disp:DisputeReasonScheme (see vocab/reasons.ttl): for example disp:not-delivered, disp:not-as-described, disp:unauthorized, disp:incorrect-amount, disp:duplicate, disp:canceled, disp:quality, disp:goodwill, and disp:other. Mappings to external frameworks (card-network chargeback families, ISO 20022 returns) are non-normative. Implementers MAY mint additional concepts in their own scheme.

disp:goodwill is refund-only and MUST NOT be used as a Dispute reason. When reason is disp:other, a free-text note SHOULD be provided to explain it.

Verification

A conformant verifier of a refund / dispute chain MUST perform the integrity, accounting, and state checks below. They are normative and exercised by the reference implementation (spec/verify.py).

  • Proofs & signers. Every object's proof MUST verify, signed by the expected party: Refund by the payee; Dispute by the payer; DisputeEvidence by its submittedBy (matching role); DisputeResolution by resolvedBy per resolverRole (an arbiter resolution by the dispute's arbiter); Reversal by the wallet; ReversalAcknowledgement by the payer.
  • Binding. Every *Digest MUST equal the JCS content digest of the referenced object, and payer / payee / currency MUST match the contested original.
  • Accounting. A return amount / resolvedAmount MUST lie within the original (with resolvedAmount ≤ disputedAmount); a Reversal's amount MUST equal its trigger; and the cumulative settled value returned against one original execution MUST NOT exceed that original's amount.
  • State. A dispute-caused Reversal MUST reference a resolution with outcome upheld or partial; an arbiter resolution MUST supersede a prior payee resolution; and rejected / withdrawn resolutions MUST carry a zero resolvedAmount and have no Reversal.

Security and Privacy

Reverse-flow value movement does not consume a Spending Authorization Credential; none of these objects are authorization instances. Integrity of a refund or dispute chain rests on the per-object proofs and the IRI-plus-digest binding to the contested payment objects. Verifiers MUST reject a Reversal whose cumulative settled value against one original execution would exceed that original's amount.

Cross-bundle no-double-refund. Value can also flow back on-chain via the AVP-Micro Settlement bundle (a reverse settlement or an EscrowRefund). When a Reversal is settled on a rail, its settlementRef SHOULD name the settling SettlementProof and carry a settlementRefDigest equal to that proof's JCS digest, so the link is authenticated rather than an opaque string. A verifier accounting for refunds against one original execution MUST count BOTH disputes Reversals and settlement reverse/refund records, so the total returned value cannot exceed the settled amount by splitting it across the two bundles.
