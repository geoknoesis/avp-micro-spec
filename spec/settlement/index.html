AVP-Micro On-Chain Settlement Binding — part of AVP-Micro (Agent Verifiable Micropayments) — covers the one step that the [[AVP-MICRO]] Payments specification deliberately scopes out: the movement of value on a public blockchain. This bundle binds AVP-Micro payments to three normative rail profiles — EVM stablecoin (USDC on an L2), Coinbase x402, and Bitcoin Lightning — through a rail-agnostic core of signed objects (SettlementInstruction, SettlementProof, PayeeAccountBinding) and an optional escrow lifecycle (EscrowLock, EscrowRelease, EscrowRefund). It rides on [[AVP-MICRO]] and [[DSA]] by reference, modifying neither. Every settlement object is secured with the mandatory ecdsa-jcs-2022 cryptosuite ([[DSA]]).

Introduction

[[AVP-MICRO]] is the trust and authorization layer for agent payments. Settlement — the actual movement of value between parties — is explicitly scoped out of that specification: it is the one money-touching step. This bundle defines how a public blockchain plugs into the settlement seam that already exists in [[AVP-MICRO]] without modifying any Payments object or its signed vectors. The binding is additive: a SettlementInstruction references a PaymentAuthorization by its IRI and a content digest, and a SettlementProof is referenced by PaymentExecution.settlementRef.

Two hard differences from the in-memory simulation ledger motivate the design (see design document): on-chain finality is asynchronous and probabilistic (block reorganizations can occur), and on-chain settlement is irreversible (there is no clawback). These two realities drive the mandatory finality state machine and the compensating- transfer reversal model described in this specification.

The CAIP-2, CAIP-10, and CAIP-19 chain-agnostic standards are used throughout to identify chains, accounts, and assets in a rail-neutral way.

Conformance

As well as sections marked as non-normative, all authoring guidelines, diagrams, examples, and notes in this specification are non-normative. Everything else is normative.

The key words MUST, MUST NOT, SHOULD, MAY, and OPTIONAL in this document are to be interpreted as described in [[RFC2119]] [[RFC8174]].

Terminology

SettlementInstruction
A wallet-signed instruction that binds a PaymentAuthorization to a concrete rail, recipient account, on-chain asset, and base-unit amount. It is the normative bridge between the AVP-Micro authorization layer and the chain.
SettlementProof
A wallet-signed attestation that settlement occurred on-chain, carrying the chain-native transaction reference, the settled base-unit amount, and a finality state. PaymentExecution.settlementRef resolves to this object's id.
PayeeAccountBinding
A payee-signed assertion that a DID controls a specific on-chain account (CAIP-10) on a specific chain (CAIP-2). Used when the payee's DID is not a did:pkh that encodes the account directly.
EscrowLock
A wallet-signed record that funds were locked in an escrow contract (or a Lightning hold-invoice) pending delivery of a service.
EscrowRelease
A wallet-signed record releasing a prior EscrowLock to the payee upon confirmed delivery, carrying the settling SettlementProof.
EscrowRefund
A wallet-signed record refunding a prior EscrowLock to the payer on timeout or dispute, carrying the settling SettlementProof.
finality
The settlement certainty state of a SettlementProof. One of three values: pending (broadcast; not yet included in a block), probabilistic (included but not yet at the confirmation threshold), or final (confirmation threshold reached, or preimage revealed for Lightning).
CAIP-2
Chain Agnostic Improvement Proposal 2: a compact identifier for a blockchain network (e.g., eip155:8453 for Base mainnet).
CAIP-10
Chain Agnostic Improvement Proposal 10: a compact identifier for an on-chain account, combining a CAIP-2 chain id with an account address (e.g., eip155:8453:0xd8dA…).
CAIP-19
Chain Agnostic Improvement Proposal 19: a compact identifier for an on-chain asset type (e.g., eip155:8453/erc20:0x833589fCD6eDb6E08f4c7C32D4f71b54bdA02913 for USDC on Base).

Securing mechanism

Every settlement object defined in this specification MUST carry an ecdsa-jcs-2022 DataIntegrityProof as defined in [[DSA]]. The proof MUST have proofPurpose assertionMethod.

A PayeeAccountBinding MUST be signed by the DID named in its subject field (the payee), using a verification method controlled by that DID.

Every other settlement object (SettlementInstruction, SettlementProof, EscrowLock, EscrowRelease, EscrowRefund) MUST be signed by the wallet: the same key that signs the PaymentExecution in [[AVP-MICRO]].

The chain-native signature (e.g., the EVM transaction signature or the Lightning payment preimage) lives inside the referenced on-chain transaction. The AVP settlement object attests to that transaction; it does not replace the chain-native proof.

Settlement objects use a five-entry @context array, in this exact order: 

[
  "https://www.w3.org/ns/credentials/v2",
  "https://w3id.org/security/data-integrity/v2",
  "https://w3id.org/spending-authority/v1",
  "https://w3id.org/avp-micro/v1",
  "https://w3id.org/avp-micro/settlement/v1"
]

This ordering ensures that credential, data-integrity, [[DSA]], [[AVP-MICRO]], and settlement terms all resolve correctly.

Core binding

A SettlementInstruction is the normative bridge from the AVP-Micro authorization layer to a concrete on-chain rail.

A SettlementInstruction MUST reference the PaymentAuthorization being settled via the authorization field (the authorization IRI) and the authorizationDigest field (the JCS content digest of the signed authorization object), pinning it to that exact signed version.

The amount and currency fields of a SettlementInstruction MUST equal the corresponding fields of the referenced PaymentAuthorization. A verifier MUST reject a SettlementInstruction whose amount or currency diverges from the authorization.

The amountBase field MUST be an integer string expressing the amount in the base (minor) units of the settlement asset. For stablecoin assets it MUST equal amount × 10decimals, computed exactly; if the decimal amount is not representable in base units the instruction MUST be rejected (no silent rounding). For rails where the quote currency differs from the settlement asset (e.g., USD quoted but settled in millisatoshi), an explicit rate field MUST be present recording the agreed conversion, and both the decimal and base-unit figures MUST be retained.

The confirmationThreshold field MUST be present and specifies the number of block confirmations required for the SettlementProof to reach finality == "final" on confirmation-based rails. For Lightning, confirmationThreshold MUST be 0 (finality is achieved by preimage reveal, not by block depth).

The mode field MUST be present and MUST be one of direct or escrow.

The rail field MUST be a concept IRI in the extensible SKOS scheme stl:RailScheme (see vocab/rails.ttl): stl:rail-evm-stablecoin, stl:rail-x402, or stl:rail-lightning. Implementers MAY mint additional rail concepts in their own scheme.

A SettlementInstruction MUST carry a nonce and an expires timestamp for freshness. Example vector: 41-settlement-instruction-evm.json.

Account binding

[[AVP-MICRO]] identifies parties by DID; public blockchains identify accounts by chain-native addresses. Without a verifiable link between a payee DID and a payeeAccount, an attacker could substitute an account and redirect funds while the authorization still verifies. This section mandates a binding.

The payeeAccount in a SettlementInstruction MUST be bound to the payee DID by exactly one of the following mechanisms:

  1. did:pkh identity. The payee DID is a did:pkh URI encoding the CAIP-10 account directly (e.g., did:pkh:eip155:8453:0xd8dA…). In this case the binding is tautological: verifying the DID resolves to the account. No additional object is required.
  2. PayeeAccountBinding. The payee (or an issuer acting on the payee's behalf) produces a PayeeAccountBinding signed by the subject DID, asserting that it controls the named account on the named chain. The SettlementInstruction references this binding via the payeeAccountBinding field (its IRI).

A verifier MUST reject a SettlementInstruction whose payeeAccount is not covered by one of the two mechanisms above. Example vector: 40-payee-account-binding.json.

Finality

A SettlementProof carries a finality field whose value MUST be one of pending, probabilistic, or final.

A fulfilled PaymentReceipt MUST NOT be issued before the associated SettlementProof has reached finality == "final". Issuing a receipt before finality exposes the payee to reorg risk: a block reorganization could reverse the on-chain transfer after the service has been delivered.

The finality transition rules per rail are:

  • Confirmation-based rails (EVM stablecoin, x402): a SettlementProof reaches final when confirmations ≥ confirmationThreshold, where confirmationThreshold is the value declared in the SettlementInstruction. Intermediate proofs with 0 < confirmations < confirmationThreshold SHOULD carry finality == "probabilistic". A freshly broadcast transaction not yet included in a block SHOULD carry finality == "pending".
  • Bitcoin Lightning: a SettlementProof reaches final when sha256(preimage) == transaction, where transaction is the Lightning payment_hash and preimage is the revealed payment preimage. Lightning payment is atomic and single-shot; there is no intermediate probabilistic state.

Example vectors advancing finality: 42-settlement-proof-evm.json (EVM, final), 44-settlement-proof-x402.json (x402, final), 47-settlement-proof-lightning.json (Lightning, final with preimage).

Escrow profile

The escrow profile is OPTIONAL. It applies when a SettlementInstruction carries mode == "escrow".

The escrow lifecycle consists of three objects:

  1. An EscrowLock is created first. It MUST reference the SettlementInstruction via instruction and instructionDigest, carry a lockRef (the chain-specific escrow handle: contract address plus escrow ID for EVM escrow contracts, or the Lightning hold-invoice payment_hash), the lockedAmountBase, and a timeout after which the lock may be refunded.
  2. On confirmed delivery of the service, an EscrowRelease MUST reference the lock via lock and lockDigest, and MUST carry a final SettlementProof referenced via settlementProof and settlementProofDigest. The referenced SettlementProof MUST have finality == "final".
  3. On timeout or dispute, an EscrowRefund MUST reference the lock via lock and lockDigest, carry a final SettlementProof via settlementProof and settlementProofDigest, and include a reason string (e.g., "timeout"). The referenced SettlementProof MUST have finality == "final".

Example vectors: 46-escrow-lock-lightning.json, 48-escrow-release-lightning.json, 49-settlement-instruction-evm-escrow.json, 50-escrow-lock-evm.json, 51-settlement-proof-evm-refund.json, 52-escrow-refund-evm.json.

Reversal

On-chain settlement is irreversible: once a transaction is final on the chain there is no mechanism to clawback funds. A reversal in the [[AVP-DISPUTES]] sense MUST therefore be realized as a new compensating transfer on-chain, with the payer and payee roles swapped (payee sends back to payer), producing its own SettlementInstruction and SettlementProof.

A Reversal object in the [[AVP-DISPUTES]] bundle references this compensating settlement via its settlementRef field, which resolves to the reverse SettlementProof's id. The [[AVP-DISPUTES]] bundle is not modified by this specification: the by-reference discipline applies symmetrically.

Example vectors: 53-reverse-settlement-instruction.json, 54-reverse-settlement-proof.json.

Rail profiles

This section defines three normative rail profiles. Each profile specifies the permitted values for chain, payeeAccount, asset, the SettlementProof transaction evidence, and the finality model. The following table provides an overview; the subsections are authoritative.

rail stl:rail-evm-stablecoin stl:rail-x402 stl:rail-lightning
chain (CAIP-2) eip155:* eip155:* (under HTTP 402) LN network id
payeeAccount CAIP-10 0x… x402 accepts → CAIP-10 BOLT11 invoice / LNURL / node pubkey
asset / base unit CAIP-19 ERC-20 / minor units CAIP-19 ERC-20 / minor units native / millisatoshi
SettlementProof.transaction tx hash + confirmations facilitator settle response (tx hash) payment_hash + preimage
Finality block depth / finalized tag same as EVM preimage revealed (single-shot final)

EVM stablecoin

The EVM stablecoin profile is the reference profile and has the broadest tooling support.

rail: MUST be stl:rail-evm-stablecoin.

chain: MUST be a CAIP-2 id with namespace eip155 (e.g., eip155:8453 for Base mainnet, eip155:137 for Polygon).

payeeAccount: MUST be a CAIP-10 account address on the declared chain (e.g., eip155:8453:0xd8dA…). The account binding requirement from applies.

asset: MUST be a CAIP-19 ERC-20 asset identifier (e.g., eip155:8453/erc20:0x833589fCD6eDb6E08f4c7C32D4f71b54bdA02913 for USDC on Base). The token's decimals determine the amountBase calculation.

SettlementProof: MUST carry a transaction (the EVM transaction hash, 0x…), a blockHeight, and a confirmations count. finality advances from pending to probabilistic to final as confirmations accumulate. A confirmationThreshold of 6 is the conventional safe default for EVM chains; profiles targeting chains with fast finality tags (safe/finalized) MAY use confirmationThreshold == 1.

Escrow: an EVM escrow contract provides the lock/release/refund mechanism. The lockRef MUST encode the contract address and the per-escrow identifier. The interface is informative; this specification does not mandate a specific contract.

Example vectors: 41-settlement-instruction-evm.json, 42-settlement-proof-evm.json, 49-settlement-instruction-evm-escrow.json, 50-escrow-lock-evm.json.

Coinbase x402

The x402 profile maps the AVP-Micro payment quote to an HTTP 402 challenge/response flow. The facilitator's on-chain ERC-20 transfer is the settlement event.

rail: MUST be stl:rail-x402.

chain: MUST be a CAIP-2 id with namespace eip155, consistent with the x402 accepts object returned by the payee server.

payeeAccount: MUST be the CAIP-10 account from the x402 accepts response (payTo field). The account binding requirement from applies.

asset: MUST be the CAIP-19 ERC-20 asset from the x402 accepts response, consistent with the [[AVP-MICRO]] quote currency.

SettlementProof: MUST carry the facilitator's on-chain settle transaction hash as transaction, and a blockHeight and confirmations count. The finality model is identical to the EVM stablecoin profile.

Escrow: x402 is pay-then-serve by design; escrow is opt-in and follows the EVM escrow contract mechanism when used.

Example vectors: 43-settlement-instruction-x402.json, 44-settlement-proof-x402.json.

Bitcoin Lightning

The Lightning profile settles in millisatoshi. Because the quote currency is typically USD and the settlement asset is native Bitcoin, the rate field is mandatory in this profile.

rail: MUST be stl:rail-lightning.

chain: MUST be the Lightning network identifier (e.g., lightning:mainnet).

payeeAccount: MUST be a BOLT11 invoice, an LNURL endpoint, or a node public key, as appropriate to the payment flow. Note: a BOLT11 invoice is single-use; implementations SHOULD use LNURL-pay or a node key for reusable endpoints.

asset: native Bitcoin; base unit is millisatoshi. amountBase MUST be the amount in millisatoshi. A 1:1 USD↔USDC assumption is not applicable here; the rate field (USD per BTC, or USD per msat) MUST be present.

confirmationThreshold: MUST be 0. Lightning finality is not confirmation-based.

SettlementProof: MUST carry the payment_hash as transaction and the payment preimage. A verifier MUST confirm sha256(preimage) == transaction and set finality == "final" upon this check passing. The blockHeight and confirmations fields MUST NOT be present (Lightning has no blocks).

Escrow: Lightning hold-invoices are the native escrow mechanism. The lockRef in an EscrowLock MUST be the hold-invoice payment_hash. Revealing the preimage (settling the hold invoice) constitutes both the EscrowRelease trigger and the finality event.

Example vectors: 45-settlement-instruction-lightning.json, 46-escrow-lock-lightning.json, 47-settlement-proof-lightning.json, 48-escrow-release-lightning.json.

Security considerations

Payment redirection

Without a verifiable binding between the payee DID and the payeeAccount, an attacker who can modify a SettlementInstruction in transit could substitute an account they control. The proof on the SettlementInstruction binds its content (including payeeAccount) to the wallet's key, preventing post-signing substitution. The account binding requirement in ensures that even a freshly constructed instruction cannot name an arbitrary account: the account must either be encoded in the payee DID (did:pkh) or covered by a payee-signed PayeeAccountBinding.

Reorg and finality risk

Block reorganizations can reverse confirmed transactions on EVM chains. The finality state machine in mitigates this by prohibiting a fulfilled receipt until the SettlementProof reaches finality == "final". Choosing a sufficiently large confirmationThreshold reduces reorg risk; chains that expose a cryptographic finality tag (e.g., the Ethereum finalized checkpoint) provide stronger guarantees than count- based thresholds. Lightning payments do not have reorg risk.

Amount precision and FX

Silent rounding of the amountBase field can cause the payer to pay a slightly different amount than the quoted decimal. The prohibition on silent rounding in prevents this. When an explicit rate is used (e.g., for Lightning), both parties SHOULD agree on the rate before signing, and verifiers MUST check the consistency of amount, rate, and amountBase.

Irreversibility and reversal

On-chain settlement cannot be clawbacked. A reversal (whether triggered by a voluntary refund or an upheld dispute) must be realized as a new compensating transfer, as described in . Implementations MUST NOT treat on-chain settlement as undoable; any system expecting reversibility must use the escrow profile and refund path.

Context protection and term reuse

Both the [[DSA]] context and this settlement context are declared with @protected: true. Settlement objects reuse [[DSA]] and [[AVP-MICRO]] terms (asset, account, amount, currency, payer, payee, authorization, execution, timestamp) rather than redefining them; those terms resolve to their upstream namespaces (avp: or dsa:). New terms introduced by this bundle expand to stl: (https://w3id.org/avp-micro/settlement/v1#).

Implementers composing settlement objects with [[AVP-DISPUTES]] objects in a single JSON-LD document SHOULD be aware that both bundles define a reason term and an authorizationDigest term. In the five-entry context array prescribed by this specification, reason resolves to stl:reason and authorizationDigest resolves to stl:authorizationDigest; in a disputes context the same term names resolve differently. Avoid merging the two context arrays into a single document; reference each bundle's objects by IRI instead.
